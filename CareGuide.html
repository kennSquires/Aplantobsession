House Plant Care Guide

Resources: Plant House Plant: Choosing, Styling, Caring By Gynelle Leon

Fiddle Leaf Fig
(Ficus lyrata)

Fiddle Leaf figs are currently a popular trend in the plant community because they are seen as statement plants for modern interior décor. With beauty comes pain, and fiddle leaf figs are known to be a pain to care for. Although once their care routine is understood, they can be a great plant to maintain, and watch them grow quickly when they are happy.


Care Level: Difficult

Mature Size for Indoors: 2-10 ft.

Watering: Fiddle leaf figs prefer to be watered weekly or every ten days; make sure that the first two inches of the soil is dry before watering. If the watering schedule is not consistent, it can lead to the plant developing red dots on the leaves or even dying. Please note that the plant's environment could affect the watering schedule, so always check the soil before watering.



Sunlight: There are not too many plants that enjoy direct sunlight but Ficus lyrata are one of the plants that absolutely love it! The plant can tolerate up to five hours of direct light; which can help encourage the plant to develop new growth. It is usually recommended to have the placed close to a south or west facing window for the best results.



Humidity/Temperature: The ideal humidity level for Ficus lyrata is in between 30-65%. If the plant's environment is on the dryer side, you may need to provide extra humidity by misting the plant or placing a humidifier in the location. It is also recommended to purchase a humidifier detector to monitor the humidity levels. For the plant to thrive in its conditions, it is usually recommended to have its environment at a temperature between 15-30 Celsius. At that temperature it will allow the plant to think it is in the right condition to grow all year round. It will also prevent it from dying; anything colder could risk losing the plant.



Fertilizer needs: fertilizing is essential for plants and their growth; it provides the plant with certain nutrients to develop healthy and dense foliage. Any type of boost from fertilizer is beneficial for the Fiddle leaf fig, although they do perform better on a fertilizer with a 3-1-2 N-P-K ratio (3% nitrogen, 1% phosphorus, 2% potassium) the numbers will be displayed on the package of the fertilizer. Depending on the type of fertilizer will determine how often to fertilize; if using a pellet form it will only need to be done every few months. While liquid fertilizers will usually have instructions on how often to do it, it is usually recommended to use it every two weeks.



Toxicity to pets: Fiddle leaf figs can be mildly toxic to pets if the plant is indigested. They can show signs of oral irritation, drooling, etc.; Call your veterinarian if you are to see any of those signs. Please make sure to keep the plant at a location where your pet cannot reach it if they have a bad habit of eating plants.



Extra Care Tips: as stated earlier, Fiddle leaf figs can be a precise plant to care for. So, we wanted to share some extra tips our company has learned over the years to successfully grow a fiddle leaf fig.

Wiping the leaves with a damp paper towel and a small amount of dish soap: this extra step will benefit your Ficus lyrata. They love having clean leaves; if there is dust build up, it can prevent your plant from taking in enough light to provide for its new growth. It also prevents any bugs developing on your plant; dish soap works as a great deterrent for most house bugs. We have been doing this once a month and have seen improvement in the foliage of our fiddle leaf figs; more dense and greener foliage!

Rotating the pot: With the plant being in direct light it tends to move towards the light quite quickly; if it does not get rotated the stem will start to bend and result in your Ficus lyrata not having a sturdy base. We tend to slightly rotate our Fiddle leaf figs every watering; so, about every two weeks.

Repotting: it is hard to give an exact time of when to repot your fiddle leaf fig; because they grow at different rates due to the environment, they are in. Although it is recommended to only repot their ‘growing’ season; during springtime. Repotting in the right weather conditions will help your Ficus lyrata recover from potential shock. Your new pot should not be any bigger than 2-4" than your previous pot it was in. Once you have repotted your Ficus lyrata make sure to give it a generous amount of water. We have found that repotting our Fiddle leaf figs every two years has been beneficial for the environment it is in.

Rubber Plant
(Ficus Elastica)

Looking for a plant with large shiny leaves, wider color selection and low maintenance, look no further! The rubber plant is an easy to care for plant that can bring great character to any room; whether you are looking for hues of ruby or something dark. Another great feature of the Ficis Elastica is the easy ability to watch new growth flourish with little attention.


Care Level: Easy

Mature Size for Indoors: 2-10 ft.

Watering: Although the rubber plant is quite forgiving when it comes to care, it can be fussy when it comes to the moisture level in the soil; not too wet and not too dry! It is recommended to water the plant every seven to fourteen days, depending on the humidity conditions within the environment. A good way to indicate whether the plant needs to be watered is checking the first inch of soil, if it is dry then it is a good indicator to water the plant. Do not worry though, if any errors are made with the watering schedule; rubber figs are hardy and can easily bounce back.



Sunlight: Rubber figs prefer bright indirect light for the best growing results; which tends to be a south or west facing window. Although they can tolerate lower light levels but expect a slower rate when it comes to growth of the plant. If the plant were to receive direct sunlight it could have the consequences of burning the leaves.



Humidity/Temperature: A great characteristic of the Ficus Elastica is that humidity levels do not seem to affect them as much. Although for keeping any tropical plant happy, it is recommended to have humidity levels between 45-60%. While the temperature in your environment should never be lower than 20 degrees Celsius; not meeting the required conditions could result in the plant dying. For the best results we propose purchasing a humidifier and temperature detector for reading the environmental conditions.



Fertilizer needs: It is usually recommended to fertilize rubber plants every 4-6 weeks using a liquid fertilizer; if you use pellets make sure to fertilize the plant less often. If the plant is over fertilized it can lead to it burning or losing the leaves. Fertilizer properties that contain 24-8-16 N-P-K ratio (24% nitrogen, 8% phosphorus, 16% potassium) will be the most beneficial for optimal growth.



Toxicity to pets: Ficus Elastica can be toxic to pets, either by indigestion or even skin contact. It can result in dermatitis, excessive drooling, oral irritation and vomiting; Call your veterinarian if you are to see any of those signs. Please make sure to keep the plant at a location where your pet cannot reach it if they have a bad habit of playing with plants.



Extra Care Tips: Although the plant is easy to care for, giving them some extra care can go a long way; here are some tricks we have learned!

Wiping the leaves with a damp paper towel and a small amount of dish soap: This extra step will benefit your rubber plant. They love having clean leaves; if there is dust build up, it can prevent your plant from taking in enough light to provide for its new growth. It also prevents any bugs developing on your plant; dish soap works as a great deterrent for most house bugs. We have been doing this once a month and have seen improvement in the foliage; more dense and greener leaves!

Potting conditions: Ficus Elastica can develop root rot if they are getting watered too much. An effective way to avoid root rot is providing the plant with a well-draining soil; this can be achieved by using an indoor potting mix with bark and charcoal mixed in. As well as using a terracotta pot (because the material allows the pot to dry out quicker compared to ceramic or plastic potting) or finding a pot with efficient drainage.

Weeping Fig
(Ficus benjamina)

Weeping figs are another well-known statement piece, like their family member; Fiddle Leaf Figs. Ficus benjamina are known for their dark bark stems and oval shaped leaves; as well as their ability to easily train into ornamental shapes. This tree that can take many different forms; has a simple care guide to follow to allow individuals to grow a healthy center piece.


Care Level: Moderate

Mature Size for Indoors: 2-6 ft.

Watering: Weeping figs prefer to have a consistent moisture level in their soil; although be aware of the possibility of over-watering the plant. These plants are more prone to root rot; which can lead to an unhealthy or dead plant. It is usually recommended to water every five to seven days; an effective way to determine is by checking to see if the first inch of the soil is dry. A reminder that everyone's water schedule can vary due to the environment the plant is in.



Sunlight: Ficus benjamina enjoys an environment that entails plenty of indirect sunlight; if the light is too direct, it could result in burning the leaves. Weeping figs can withstand lower light levels as well but please note it may take longer to see growth and the plant may require more fertilizer.



Humidity/Temperature: Although weeping figs are not too fussy when it comes to their light source, they are quite demanding when it comes to humidity and temperature levels. They prefer to have a humidity level between 50-80% and should not reach a temperature lower than 20 degrees Celsius; not meeting the required conditions could result in the plant losing its leaves or ability to stay alive. It is recommended to have a moist and warm spot for your weeping fig; for best results we propose to purchase a humidifier and temperature detector for reading the environmental conditions.



Fertilizer needs: Weeping figs need consistent fertilizing to flourish as indoor plants. It is usually recommended to fertilize (with liquid fertilizer) the plant at least once a month during its growing period; which is during the summertime. Then only every two months during the other seasons. For the best results with fertilizing, it is recommended to use the fertilizer ratio; 3-1-2 N-P-K ratio (3% nitrogen, 1% phosphorus, 2% potassium), this information can be found on the back of the bottle.



Toxicity to pets: F. benjamina can be toxic to pets, either by indigestion or even skin contact. It can result in dermatitis, excessive drooling, oral irritation and vomiting; Call your veterinarian if you are to see any of those signs. Please make sure to keep the plant at a location where your pet cannot reach it if they have a bad habit of playing with plants.



Extra Care Tips: With extra love, time and secret tips can result in a beautiful weeping fig; so, we thought to share some!

Creating a fuller tree: Everyone loves to see a plant full of green leaves and branches, although the plant may not grow like that naturally. As mentioned earlier, weeping figs are easy plants to train to ornamental shape. A technique we have learned is called notching; it is when you notch the main stem with scissors or a blade. For best results, notch the stem close to a node (bulb looking figure on stem, this is usually where new growth comes from!) Once a node is located, give a generous cut into the plant. This will encourage the plant to grow a new branch; within a few weeks there should be new growth! Fun fact: this technique can be done on most plants!!

Rotating the pot: With the plant being in a brighter location it tends to move towards the light quite quickly; if it does not get rotated the stem will start to bend and result in your Weeping Fig not having a sturdy base. Rotating the plant will allow the plant to grow in a straighter direction; we slightly rotate the pot every watering session.
